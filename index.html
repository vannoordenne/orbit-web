About or.bit

or.bit

Observatory for Research in Breakthrough and Interactive Technologies

or.bit is a practice-based research institute that investigates, questions and makes technology tangible. We work at the intersection of research, design, experimentation and education.

From a transdisciplinary model, we connect critical reflection to concrete forms: publications, methods, workshops, installations, provocatypes and tools.

Three branches

Research

Investigating the relationship between technology and society. We develop publications, frameworks, methods and analyses around emerging technologies, AI, critical technology, speculative design and socio-technical systems.

Lab

Making technology tangible and experiential. We build provocatypes, installations, interactive experiments and other investigative prototypes that make complex systems feelable and discussable.

Educatie

Developing workshops, trainings and learning formats that help people engage critically with technology. We translate complex technological systems into understandable, experiential and discussable formats for students, professionals and public contexts.

or.bit focuses on emerging and interactive technologies, with particular attention to AI, digital systems, infrastructures, interfaces and their societal implications.

Lab

Lab

In the Lab we make technology tangible and experiential. We build provocatypes, installations, interactive experiments and other investigative prototypes that make complex systems feelable and discussable.

The Lab is where thinking becomes material — and where material asks new questions.

Output

Provocatypes Installations Experiments Critical making Speculative design Physical computing Interactive prototypes
What is a provocatype?

A provocatype is a prototype designed not to be used, but to provoke. It makes a system visible, a consequence tangible, an assumption discussable. It is research made physical.

Dark Tech Toolkit

PROTOTYPE IN DEVELOPMENT

The Dark Tech Toolkit is a physical toolkit for critically investigating technology. It helps users make digital systems, AI and technological infrastructures discussable, tangible and analysable.

The toolkit is designed as a hands-on instrument — something you can pick up, spread on a table and use to think together. Not a manual. Not a textbook. A set of tools.

Contents (provisional)

■ Analysis cards for mapping technological systems
■ Discussion formats and facilitation guides
■ Speculation prompts and scenario exercises
■ Ethical matrices and decision frameworks
■ Case study materials
■ Reference sheets and concept overviews

Who is it for?

■ Educators and workshop facilitators
■ Students in technology, design and social sciences
■ Organisations wanting to think critically about their technology use
■ Researchers and curious practitioners
